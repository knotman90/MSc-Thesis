Bus-based networks

The most simple family of topologies that give birth to the bus-based networks. Each node is connected to a single shared medium that is common to all the nodes. The most important advantage of bus is that the distance of two nodes is constant O(1) and the cost scale linearly as the number of nodes p7. A message from the source is broadcasted to all machines connected to the bus and every machine ignore the message except for the intended address for the message that accept the data. The low cost of implementing this topology if tradeoff by the difficulties in managing it and plus, because of the bounded bandwidth of buses, typical bus-based machine are limited to dozen of nodes.
