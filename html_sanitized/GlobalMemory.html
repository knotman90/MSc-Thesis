Global Memory

The most general space allowing both, reading and writing of data, is the global memory. It is allocated and is allocated and managed by the host. Due to DRAM bandwidth optimizations and the GPU architecture, and, as each multiprocessor works independently, unordered reads and writes are possible because all threads are able to read from all memory spaces at any time without any mutual exclusivity mechanism. Is up to the programmer to avoid race-conditions and incoherences.
