Multiple Rectangular Bounding Boxes

RBB as seen in section [sect:RBBOptimization] (at page ) is a valid solution that permits a good gain in performance. However, in some situations, it does fit the space of active cells in a inefficient way. Real occupancy of the device is measured by the ratio \(\eta=\frac{ACTIVE\_CELLS}{IDLE\_CELLS}\). For example, figure [fig:activeIdleRatio] shows how \(\eta\) decreases over time.

Multiple RBB on the same cellular space may lead to higher values of \(\eta\) (see figure [fig:MultiRBBExample]) during the execution. Here, the large single RBB was divided in two smaller RBB, with the property that all the active cells are inside their boundaries.
